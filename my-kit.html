Filming & Photography

NiceVeedi
2-Pack LED Video Light Kit
Bi-color range from 2800K to 6500K covers everything from warm tungsten to daylight, which matters when you’re mixing ambient with artificial light in a small shed. I set one as a key light and one as fill — the two-light setup kills the harsh shadows that make close-up blade and handle work impossible to read. One stand doubles as the camera mount, which keeps the footprint manageable in a tight space.
Famall
Flexible Phone Tripod with Remote
The main use is overhead flat-lay shots — before, during, and after, laid out on the cutting mat. The flexible legs wrap around a shelf bracket above the bench and hold the phone dead steady, and the remote shutter means I'm not reaching over and shaking the frame. The 1/4″ thread takes a GoPro or small camera body when the phone isn't the right tool for the shot.
DJI
Mic Mini — 1TX + 1RX
Planned for shop narration — clips to a shirt, transmits wirelessly to a single receiver that connects directly to iPhone via USB-C. No adapter, no cable run, no mic in frame. Right now the built-in iPhone mic is handling narration, which means positioning the phone close enough to pick up voice and far enough to get the shot. It’s a compromise. This solves it.
Planned — not yet purchased · using built-in mic in the meantime

Finishing & Restoration

Jesverty
DC Power Supply Variable (0–32V, 0–10A)
The workhorse for electrolytic rust removal. Set it around 3V and let the process run overnight — iron oxide reverses itself without touching the metal. The 0–32V range and 10A ceiling cover everything from a small chisel to a full plane body, and the fine-adjustment encoder means you can dial in exactly what the setup needs.
Walrus Oil
Polymerized Tung Oil
My go-to finish for tool handles. Penetrates deep, cures hard, and gives a warm, natural sheen that doesn't look plastic. Three coats over 48–72 hours.
Used on: Mac Tools screwdriver set
Fiebing's
Leather Dye
Unconventional on tool handles — but absolutely worth it. Apply Black first, then Dark Brown, then Mahogany for dimensional color that looks like it comes from inside the wood.
Used on: Mac Tools screwdriver set
Picreator
Renaissance Wax
Museum-grade microcrystalline wax. Final coat on all my restorations. Protects without changing the look — a soft, period-appropriate sheen.
Used on: All restorations
Wood Bleach
Oxalic Acid
Essential for lifting tannin staining from old tool handles. Mix with warm water, apply, let sit, neutralize. Takes wood from grey-brown back to near-original tone.
Used on: Mac Tools screwdriver set
Flitz
Multi-Purpose Metal Polish
My go-to for steel and brass on restorations. Cleans and polishes without overdoing it — a light touch goes a long way.
Used on: Bridgeport multitool
Klean-Strip
Odorless Mineral Spirits
The first step on almost every restoration — cuts through decades of grime and oils, and gives you an honest preview of what the wood will look like finished.
Used on: Bridgeport multitool
Hyde Tools
Nylon Bristle Brushes
For scrubbing metal parts with mineral spirits. Soft enough to not scratch, stiff enough to get into knurling and tight spaces.
Used on: Bridgeport multitool
Scotch-Brite
Fine Abrasive Pads (Superfine Assortment)
For addressing light surface oxidation on steel. Removes corrosion without altering the original surface finish — the right tool when restraint matters.
Used on: Bridgeport multitool
3-In-One
Multi-Purpose Oil
Light machine oil for lubricating chuck mechanisms and collet jaws after cleaning. A small amount, applied carefully, keeps things moving smoothly.
Used on: Bridgeport multitool
3M Cubitron II
Hookit Sanding Discs — 3" / 150+
Precision-shaped ceramic grain that cuts faster and lasts longer than conventional abrasives. The 3" size pairs perfectly with detail sanders. Pack of 50 with multi-hole dust extraction.
For: detail sanding and finish work
3M Cubitron II
Hookit Sanding Discs — 5" / 220+
Same Cubitron ceramic technology in a 5" format for random orbital sanders. 220+ grit is the sweet spot for finishing passes before applying dye or oil. Pack of 50.
For: pre-finish sanding on larger surfaces
3M Cubitron II
Hookit Sanding Discs — 5" Multi-Pack
Three grits in one pack — 240+, 320+, and 400+ — for working through the final sanding stages without ordering separately. The multi-hole pattern keeps the disc clean and extends its life considerably.
For: progressive finish sanding
3M Cubitron II
Hookit Sanding Discs — 5" / 80+
The aggressive grit for early-stage material removal — stripping old finish, flattening glue-ups, or cutting through rust and grime. Cubitron ceramic grain works faster and stays sharper than conventional abrasives at this grit. Pack of 50.
For: aggressive material removal and surface prep
3M Xtract
Net Disc 710W — 5" / 120+
The net construction is the difference — virtually dust-free sanding across the entire disc surface, not just through a few holes. 120+ grit bridges the gap between aggressive removal and finish prep. A premium option when clean air matters. Pack of 50.
For: stock removal, intermediate sanding, and fine finishing

Many of these tools were handed down to me by my father when he passed. Back then I didn't have the appreciation for them that I do now — I'm sure I let some real gems go that I would have loved to keep. Hindsight is 20/20.

My current workspace is a roughly 8’ × 7’ shed. I run an extension cord for power. No heat. No installed lights. I often find myself sitting in the backyard hoping the notorious Seattle rain stays away while I plug away on a project.

This spring (2026) I’m tearing it down and building a real workshop — roughly 9’ × 10’ with windows, proper power (including 220V), air filtration, insulation, and real tool storage. I’m documenting the whole thing publicly. Follow the shed upgrade →

Hand Planes — Vintage Stanley Bailey

Jack Plane

Black painted hardwood tote and knob. The finish plane — takes shavings thin enough to read through. Still one of the best tools in the shop.
