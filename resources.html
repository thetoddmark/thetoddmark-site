Resources & Influences

These are the creators, historians, and craftspeople who taught me most of what I know about hand tools, tool restoration, and working with wood. If you're new to this world, this is an excellent place to start.

01

Restoration & Tool History

Creators focused on rescuing, restoring, and understanding vintage hand tools — and the history behind them.

Hand Tool Rescue
YOUTUBE · @HandToolRescue
Highly detailed hand tool restorations — systematic, methodical, often funny, and very satisfying to watch.
What I learned: Even tools that look beyond saving often have years of life left if you're patient, creative, and really want to see the tool brought back to its former glory (often well beyond that too!)
Antique & Vintage Restorations
YOUTUBE · @AntiqueVintageRestorations
Rescuing vintage and antique tools through careful cleaning, rust removal, and patient restoration work.
What I learned: Patience and chemical knowledge are more valuable than elbow grease. The right process, applied calmly, is everything.
Just Plane Fun
YOUTUBE · @JustPlaneFun
Vintage tool restoration and hand plane deep dives. Exactly the kind of content that got me started on this path.
What I learned: Understanding Stanley type studies completely changed how I shop at flea markets and estate sales.
357magdad
YOUTUBE · @357magdad
Deep-dive vintage tool restorations as well as fun restorations of other vintage things like old toys — watch a few and get hooked… Chuck is a funny mascot too.
What I learned: There's always another level of quality hiding inside a beat-up old tool. Don't give up on the ugly ones.
Lost Art Press
WEB · lostartpress.com
Publisher of some of the best hand tool woodworking books in print. Chris Schwarz and company — serious scholarship on craft, tools, and technique. The bookshelf standard.
What I learned: Reading the history behind your tools changes how you use them. Context is part of the craft.
02

Hand Tool Masters / Education

Master craftsmen and dedicated teachers whose instruction forms the backbone of a hand tool education.

Paul Sellers
YOUTUBE · @Paul.Sellers
Master craftsman and educator. Clean, precise hand tool work with a thoughtful, philosophical approach to the craft.
What I learned: The cut doesn't have to be perfect — it has to be intentional. Sellers made me slow down and think before I cut.
Rob Cosman
YOUTUBE · @RobCosman
World-class hand tool craftsman best known for his dovetail instruction — precise, methodical, and deeply educational.
What I learned: I learn something new from Rob with every video. I also really admire his Purple Heart Project for wounded warriors — thepurpleheartproject.org
Rex Krueger
YOUTUBE · @RexKrueger
Low-cost, high-skill woodworking. Rex makes the case that you don't need expensive tools to do excellent work.
What I learned: You don't need expensive tools to do excellent work. Rex taught me that skill and patience outweigh kit every time.
Wood By Wright
YOUTUBE · Wood By Wright
Hand tool focused, deeply knowledgeable. One of the best channels for understanding how and why hand tools work.
What I learned: Understanding why a hand tool works — the geometry, the physics — makes you a better craftsman than any technique video alone.
The Wood Whisperer
YOUTUBE · @woodwhisperer
Marc Spagnuolo brings a grounded, no-nonsense approach to furniture woodworking and shop craft. Excellent production, thoughtful instruction, and a genuine respect for both hand and power tools. One of the most trusted voices in the online woodworking community.
What I learned: That clean technique and good habits matter far more than expensive tools — and that you can build beautiful furniture in a modest shop.
ENCurtis
YOUTUBE · @ENCurtis
Entertaining and informative woodworking projects.
What I learned: I'm wildly jealous of his workshop and tools — not to mention his skills. Oh, and I want his coffee mug!!
03

Modern Woodworking Creators

Contemporary woodworkers blending hand tool sensibility with a modern approach to the craft.

Stumpy Nubs
YOUTUBE · @StumpyNubs
Practical, approachable, wide-ranging. An excellent starting point for anyone interested in getting into hand and power tool woodworking.
What I learned: James's approachable style is thoroughly enjoyable and informative — my only issue is that I wish he'd post a new video every day, because I've yet to find one that didn't teach me something new.
3×3 Custom
YOUTUBE · @3x3CustomTamar
Tamar Hannah — jigs, fixtures, and smart shop solutions. Brings a fresh perspective to woodworking content.
What I learned: A well-made jig isn't a shortcut — it's a sign you understand the problem deeply. Tamar's shop solutions changed how I think about setup time.
Bourbon Moth Woodworking
YOUTUBE · Bourbon Moth Woodworking
Creative woodworking that blends hand tools and power tools without apology — focused on results and real-shop problem solving.
What I learned: Funny, creative, and deeply instructional — this hobby should be fun, and Bourbon Moth gets that.
Paoson Woodworking
YOUTUBE · @Paoson_Woodworking
Woodworker known for creative shop tools, jigs, and ingenious builds — all made from scratch.
What I learned: I have so much to learn and so much to build — this channel opened my eyes to some really creative solutions I never would have thought of on my own.
Pedulla Studio
YOUTUBE · @PedullaStudio
Fine furniture and hand tool work at a high level. Inspiring if you want to see where this craft can go.
What I learned: Designing and building fine furniture truly is an art — I am not yet an artist, but hey, learning is a process!
A Plane Life
YOUTUBE · @aplanelife688
Hand plane focused content — type studies, restoration, and honest user reviews of vintage tools.
What I learned: Hand plane type studies completely changed how I buy and evaluate vintage planes. What used to look like 'a plane' is now a specific tool with a history.
Dave Corinth
YouTube · Hand Tool Kits
Creator of the chip breaker screwdriver kit. Makes specialist hand tool components you didn't know you needed — until you do.
What I learned: Thoughtful specialty tools made by someone who actually uses them are worth every penny. His chip breaker screwdriver kit is now a permanent fixture in my restoration kit.
Gordon Addison
YOUTUBE · @GordonAddison
Thoughtful hand tool woodworking and antique knob and tote restorations.
What I learned: Calm, detailed, methodical technique is essential — worth watching Gordon's videos to see these beautiful pieces come back to life.
Why these creators?

Why these creators?

The woodworking and hand tool world is full of incredible teachers. The creators listed here consistently produce thoughtful, educational content and have helped shape my understanding of vintage tools, restoration techniques, and traditional craftsmanship.

Missing someone?
