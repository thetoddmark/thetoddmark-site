The Audience

People who actually spend money on tools.

The Todd·Mark reader isn't browsing. They're researching. They want to know what works, what doesn't, and whether a product is worth the money before they spend it. They're hobbyist woodworkers and mechanics who've already decided they care about quality — they just need help knowing which quality to buy.

These are people who look up "oxalic acid wood bleach" at 11pm, who have a wishlist of tools and a budget that's slightly smaller than they'd like, and who treat a good write-up the same way they treat a recommendation from a trusted friend in the trade. When The Todd·Mark documents a product, readers take note.

The newsletter goes to subscribers who opted in specifically to read restoration write-ups — no broad lifestyle list, no casual signups. Everyone on it wants exactly this content.

What I Offer

Honest coverage. No fluff. Real use.

Every write-up is built around actual use in an actual project. I'm not reviewing products in a vacuum — I'm using them in the middle of a full restoration and documenting what happens. That context is what makes readers trust the coverage.

How It Works

Simple. No contracts, no nonsense.

  1. 1
    You reach out
    Tell me what you make and what you'd like to see covered. I'll tell you honestly whether it fits a current or upcoming project — I won't force a product into a write-up where it doesn't belong.
  2. 2
    We agree on scope
    Tool loan, supplies send, newsletter mention, or some combination. No formal contracts for most arrangements — just a clear mutual understanding of what's happening and what I'll deliver.
  3. 3
    I use it. For real.
    The product goes into an actual project. I document as I go — photos, process notes, honest observations. Nothing is staged. If it's outstanding, I'll say so. If it has a limitation, I'll mention that too.
  4. 4
    It goes live
    The write-up publishes on The Todd·Mark with full photo documentation, a mention in the newsletter, and affiliate links where appropriate. You get a link to the live piece when it's up.
Full Disclosure

What this isn't.

I don't do guaranteed positive reviews.

If a product doesn't perform, I'll say so — diplomatically, but honestly. That's the reason readers trust the coverage, and it's the reason coverage here is worth anything. A five-star review from a site that always gives five stars is worth nothing to anyone.

What I will do: give your product fair use, full context, and a thorough write-up. Most good tools get good write-ups. That's how this works.

The site is also new. I won't pretend otherwise. What I have is a growing, engaged readership, a demonstrated commitment to quality documentation, and a format that readers return to. If you're a smaller brand looking for the kind of genuine, detailed coverage that a big-budget influencer operation won't bother with — this is probably the right conversation to have.
