The Project

A useful audience to be in front of.

The Todd·Mark is a documentation project about restoring vintage hand tools, refinishing furniture, and repurposing objects worth keeping. The people who follow along aren’t browsing for inspiration — they’re mid-project, actively looking for the right product, technique, or finish.

That’s a useful audience to be in front of.

The channel is early and growing. What it already has is a specific niche, a highly engaged viewer base, and content that doesn’t expire — a video about restoring a Stanley Bailey plane or finishing wood with OSMO oil stays relevant and searchable for years.

What Partnership Looks Like

Products that earn their place.

Partnerships that work here are ones where the product earns its place — meaning it’s something that genuinely belongs in a restoration, refinishing, or workshop context. That includes tool brands, wood finishes, cleaning and restoration products, leather care, workshop supplies, and the platforms and marketplaces where old things get found.

What’s available: dedicated product features, integrated mentions within project videos, affiliate arrangements, and product-for-content exchanges for the right fit.
